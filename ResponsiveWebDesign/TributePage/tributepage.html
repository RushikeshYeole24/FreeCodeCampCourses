Swami Vivekananda
A Visionary and Spiritual Luminary
  • 1863: January 12 - Birth of Narendranath Datta in Kolkata, India.
  • Late 1800s: Immersed in an intellectually stimulating environment, Narendranath develops a keen interest in philosophy, religion, and social issues.
  • 1881: Meets Sri Ramakrishna Paramahansa, a revered saint, and becomes his devoted disciple.
  • 1884-1886: After the passing of Sri Ramakrishna, Narendranath and a group of fellow disciples embark on a life of renunciation and begin their wandering journey across India, seeking spiritual enlightenment and serving the needy.
  • 1890: Returns to Kolkata and takes the monastic vows, assuming the name Swami Vivekananda.
  • 1891-1893: Travels extensively across India, witnessing the widespread poverty and social injustices, which deepens his resolve to work towards uplifting the masses.
  • 1893: September 11 - Swami Vivekananda delivers his historic speech at the World's Parliament of Religions in Chicago, introducing the world to the richness of Hindu philosophy and the universality of spiritual truths.
  • 1894-1896: Spends time in America, delivering lectures, conducting classes on Vedanta philosophy, and establishing the Vedanta Societies in major cities.
  • 1897: Swami Vivekananda establishes the Ramakrishna Mission, with the aim of alleviating human suffering, promoting education, and uplifting society's marginalized sections.
  • 1898: Begins a comprehensive tour of India, traveling extensively to understand the country's socio-economic conditions and inspire people towards social reforms.
  • 1899: Publishes "Raja Yoga," a seminal work on the practical aspects of yoga and meditation.
  • 1901: Passes away on July 4, leaving behind a rich legacy and a profound impact on countless lives.
  • 1914: The Belur Math, the headquarters of the Ramakrishna Mission, is consecrated in Belur, West Bengal, as a symbol of Swami Vivekananda's vision.
  • 1938: The Advaita Ashrama, a spiritual retreat center, is established in the Himalayan foothills to preserve and propagate Swami Vivekananda's teachings.
  • 20th Century: Swami Vivekananda's teachings continue to inspire individuals globally, encouraging them to seek self-realization, embrace service, and promote religious harmony.
  • 21st Century: Swami Vivekananda's message of unity, compassion, and spiritual growth remains relevant and continues to guide people in leading meaningful lives. His influence can be seen through numerous organizations, educational institutions, and social initiatives dedicated to his ideals.
